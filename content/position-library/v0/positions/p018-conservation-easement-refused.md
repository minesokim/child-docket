## What this entry IS

A REFUSED entry covering the **syndicated conservation easement** abuse pattern that the IRS has aggressively litigated since Notice 2017-10. The refusal protects the EA's PTIN from the highest-penalty preparer exposure in the modern tax landscape.

Legitimate conservation easements (e.g., a farmer donating an easement on family farmland to a qualified conservancy at FMV) are NOT covered by this refusal. Those are evaluated case-by-case and are not in v0 scope.

## Position considered (and refused)

A taxpayer (or syndicate of taxpayers) claims a charitable contribution deduction under IRC §170(h) for the donation of a "conservation easement" to a qualified organization. The deduction is typically valued at 4-10x the taxpayer's basis, generating an outsized §170 deduction.

Common syndicated patterns:
- Taxpayer invests $X in a partnership; partnership donates easement; taxpayer claims deduction of $4X-$10X
- Appraisal asserts development potential or commercial value far above what comparable sales support
- "Charity" recipient is a related-party or recently-formed shell organization
- Tax-shelter promoter solicits investors with promise of "5-to-1 tax savings"

## Why this position is refused

1. **Notice 2017-10** designates syndicated conservation easements as **listed transactions**. The IRS has specifically identified this pattern as abusive and is litigating aggressively. Hundreds of cases pending in Tax Court.
2. **§6662(h) 40% gross valuation misstatement penalty** triggers when claimed value is 200%+ of correct value. Most syndicated easements claim 400-1000%, triggering the maximum penalty.
3. **§6701 aiding penalty** applies to preparers who knew or should have known the position was wrong. Syndicated easements are well-publicized; "should have known" is the IRS's position by default.
4. **§170(h) qualification requirements** systematically fail in syndicated cases:
   - **§170(h)(1)(A) qualified real property interest** — perpetual easement, but with reversion / amendment rights that void perpetuity
   - **§170(h)(2)(C) qualified organization** — must be public charity or government entity; many "charities" in syndicates fail qualification
   - **§170(h)(4) conservation purpose** — must be one of 4 statutory purposes (preservation of land for outdoor recreation, protection of natural habitat, preservation of open space, preservation of historic structure); many syndicated easements fail this test
   - **§170(f)(11) qualified appraisal** — must be by qualified appraiser; many syndicated appraisals fail due to conflicts of interest, methodology errors, or inflated comparable sales
5. **Case law has decisively rejected syndicated easements**:
   - **Belair Woods, LLC v. Comm'r** (T.C. Memo 2018-XX) — $50M+ deduction reduced to ~$1M
   - **Glade Creek Partners v. Comm'r** (T.C. Memo 2020-XX) — full disallowance + §6662(h) penalty
   - **Plateau Holdings v. Comm'r** (T.C. Memo 2020-XX) — full disallowance + listed transaction penalty
   - **Pine Mountain Preserve v. Comm'r** (T.C. Memo 2018-XX) — substantial disallowance + valuation penalty
   - Tax Court has imposed §6662(h) 40% penalty in roughly 90% of syndicated easement cases in 2018-2025
6. **Inflation Reduction Act of 2022** added §170(h)(7), which **limits the deduction for partnership-allocated easements to 2.5x basis** (effective for contributions after 12/29/2022). This change effectively kills the syndicated structure.

## The Refusal Floor — what makes this refused vs defensible

| Factor | Legitimate (not in v0; case-by-case) | REFUSED (syndicated pattern) |
|---|---|---|
| Deduction value vs basis | At or near basis (sometimes 1.5-2x for appreciated land) | 4x+ basis claimed |
| Charity recipient | Long-established public charity (Nature Conservancy, etc.) | Newly-formed entity, related-party, or shell |
| Promoter involvement | None — taxpayer-initiated | Syndicate promoter with marketing materials |
| Appraisal | Independent qualified appraiser, no conflicts | Promoter-affiliated appraiser, inflated methodology |
| Easement perpetuity | Truly perpetual, no amendment rights | Reversion / amendment provisions that void perpetuity |
| Conservation purpose | Clearly fits one of §170(h)(4) categories | Stretched or theoretical purpose |
| Investor partners | Pre-existing co-owners | Investors recruited via tax-shelter marketing |
| Post-IRA 2022 | Subject to §170(h)(7) 2.5x basis limit | Marketed as if pre-IRA rules still apply |

If **any 3+ of the "REFUSED" criteria apply**, the position is refused. If the position is a syndicated investment promoted with tax-savings marketing, refuse unconditionally — these are explicitly listed transactions.

## Why we surface the refusal

The refusal protects the preparer in three ways:

1. **Documents due diligence**. If the IRS later challenges that the preparer should have warned the client, the refusal log demonstrates the preparer recognized the abuse pattern.
2. **Educates the client**. The client may have been pitched by a syndicate promoter; the refusal explanation shows them what the IRS sees.
3. **Demonstrates compliance-first product design**. In a competitive landscape where some AI tools surface aggressive positions, refusing the most-litigated abuse pattern is the trust artifact.

## Common red flags (always REFUSE)

- Client mentions "syndicated" or "partnership" + easement
- Client received tax-shelter marketing materials promising X-to-1 tax savings
- Appraisal value > 2x basis without explicit appreciated-property documentation
- Recipient charity was recently formed (<3 years)
- Investor partners were recruited (not pre-existing co-owners)
- Promoter is paid contingent on deduction generated
- Easement contains amendment or reversion provisions
- Donation made via 1031 exchange or similar tax-deferred mechanism
