## Position considered (and refused)

S-Corp owner attempts to rent their personal residence to their own S-Corp for ≤14 days/year at an inflated rate ($3,000-$10,000+/day) to maximize the §280A(g) exclusion + §162 deduction.

Common patterns:
- $5,000/day × 14 days = $70,000 "tax-free" income shift
- $10,000/day × 14 days = $140,000
- Used for single-attendee "board meetings" with no documented business purpose
- No comparable rental data; rate justified by "owner's hourly consulting rate" or fabricated

## Why this position is refused

The Augusta Rule itself is settled law (per p004). The **rate** is what fails:

1. **Rate is not fair market value** per Rev. Rul. 2004-32 + Sinopoli v. Comm'r (T.C. Memo 2023-105). Reasonable rent must reflect what a third party would pay for comparable space.
2. **Comp data does not support the rate**. Real conference / event venue rentals in nearly every U.S. market are $500-$2,500/day. Rates above this require very specific premium-venue comp data.
3. **Audit pattern is well-established**. The IRS routinely challenges Augusta Rule deductions when:
   - Rate exceeds local comp data by 2x+
   - Single-attendee "meetings"
   - No agendas or minutes
   - Pattern of using full 14 days every year (suggests aggressive maximization)
   - Rate justified by "owner's hourly rate" (improper comparable)
4. **§6694(b) reckless threshold is real**. Knowingly using an inflated rate when comp data is available constitutes reckless disregard. Penalty: $5,000 OR 75% of fees.
5. **§6662(a) substantial understatement** triggers at 10% of tax or $5,000 if applicable. The taxpayer carries the penalty even if the preparer escapes §6694.

## The Refusal Floor — when does the position cross from Tier 2 to REFUSED

| Factor | Tier 2 Defensible | REFUSED |
|---|---|---|
| Rate vs local comp data | Within 20% of comp data | >50% above comp data without specific premium justification |
| Documented business purpose | Real (board meeting, planning retreat, training) with agenda + minutes | Single-attendee or vague "strategy" days |
| Attendee list | Multiple attendees (board members, key staff) | Owner only |
| Comp data source | Local conference venue / event space rentals | "Owner's hourly rate" or fabricated |
| Day count | ≤ 14 with documented purposes each day | 14 every year, identical pattern |
| Personal use during rental | None | Documented personal use of residence during rental days |
| Rental agreement | Real, dated before rental | Backdated or absent |
| 1099-MISC | Issued | Not issued |
| Reasonable rent under §1.162-7 | Yes | No |

If **any 3+ of the "REFUSED" column criteria apply**, the position is refused. Document the refusal reasoning.

## What we would need to take this position

To upgrade an abusive Augusta variant back to Tier 2:

1. **Comparable rental data from premium venues**: e.g., a private conference center charging $3,000-$5,000/day for similar event space. The data must be specific, dated, and from the geographic market.
2. **Real business purpose** with agendas + minutes + attendee lists (multiple attendees).
3. **Day count below the cap with documented purpose per day** (not 14 carbon-copy days).
4. **Rental agreement signed before the rental** with FMV rate.
5. **1099-MISC issued** to the owner.
6. **No personal use during rental days**.

If all 6 are present + rate is supported by comp data, the position graduates to Tier 2 (Substantial Authority) per p004.
