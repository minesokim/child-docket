# Position p005: Home office accountable plan reimbursement for S-Corp shareholder

## Position statement

An S-Corp may reimburse its >2% shareholder, under a §62(c) accountable plan, for the regular and exclusive business use of a portion of the shareholder's personal residence. The reimbursement is deducted by the S-Corp as an ordinary §162 business expense, and excluded from the shareholder's gross income under §1.62-2 (the accountable plan exclusion).

## Plain-English description

Post-TCJA, the "deduct home office on Schedule A as an unreimbursed employee expense" mechanism is suspended through 12/31/2025. For S-Corp >2% shareholders + sole-employee shareholders, the only viable mechanism to capture the home office cost is the accountable plan reimbursement.

The mechanism works like this:

1. S-Corp adopts an accountable plan policy (board minutes / employment agreement).
2. Shareholder maintains a portion of their home (e.g., 60 sq ft in a 1,800 sq ft home = 3.3% of total square footage) used "regularly and exclusively" for business purposes per §280A(c)(1).
3. S-Corp pays a monthly reimbursement to the shareholder (e.g., $200/mo × 12 = $2,400/year) for the home office expense.
4. Reimbursement is deducted by S-Corp as §162 ordinary expense (Form 1120-S Line 17).
5. Reimbursement is excluded from shareholder's gross income under §1.62-2 (no W-2 inclusion).
6. Shareholder maintains substantiation per §1.62-2(c) requirements.

**Net effect**: deduction at corporate level + zero income at individual level + no FICA/Medicare on the reimbursement. Functionally a "tax-free" expense reimbursement.

**Critical**:
- The §280A(c)(1) **exclusive-use test** is strict. Examiners scrutinize whether the space is truly used "regularly and exclusively" for business — not occasional, not mixed-use.
- The accountable plan must be **established** under §1.62-2(c) — written policy, substantiation requirement, reasonable time-period for substantiation.
- The reimbursement amount must be **reasonable** — typically based on square-footage proportion + actual home expense (utilities, depreciation pro rata, mortgage interest pro rata, etc.) per IRS Publication 587.

**Why Tier 3 instead of Tier 1**:
The position itself (accountable plan reimbursement for home office) is statutorily settled. The **substantiation gap** is the load-bearing risk. Post-TCJA, examiners scrutinize home office accountable plans for remote-work-era abuse (e.g., taxpayer claims 100% of basement as office when half is used for laundry). The §1.62-2 substantiation requirement is where most cases fail on audit.

Form 8275 disclosure pre-empts the §6662 understatement penalty if the position is challenged.

**Why an EA cares**: many S-Corp shareholder clients want this deduction. Done sloppily, it's an audit magnet. Done well with documentation pack, it's a legitimate $2K-$5K/year reimbursement that compounds over the life of the practice.

## Fact pattern triggers

The Discovery agent should consider this position when:
- S-Corp K-1 (1120-S) where shareholder is >2% owner
- Shareholder works from home (Schedule C self-employed mention, or sole-employee structure)
- 1120-S Line 17 "Other deductions" includes reimbursement to shareholder
- Schedule A on owner's return shows no home office expense (post-TCJA suspended path)
- Owner has dedicated home office space (rough square footage observable from intake forms)

## 4-tier confidence rationale

**Tier 3 (Reasonable Basis + 8275)** for the standard fact pattern: S-Corp >2% shareholder, 60-150 sq ft home office, regular + exclusive use, written accountable plan policy, monthly reimbursement at reasonable rate.

The position is supported by clear regulatory framework + IRS guidance, but examiner scrutiny on the "regular and exclusive" test post-TCJA + remote-work era warrants disclosure. The 8275 protects the preparer from §6694(a) if the position is challenged.

**The classification downgrades or upgrades based on substantiation**:

- **Tier 2 (Substantial Authority)** if substantiation pack is fully built (square footage measurement + photographs + accountable plan policy + monthly substantiation requests + receipt records). Some practitioners argue this fact pattern is Tier 2; defaulting to Tier 3 for the conservative position + 8275 disclosure for §6662 protection.
- **Tier 4 (MLTN) or REFUSED below RB** if:
  - The "office" is a corner of a mixed-use room (fails §280A(c)(1) exclusive-use)
  - The accountable plan policy was not in place at the time of the reimbursement (must be **established** before reimbursement)
  - The reimbursement rate is grossly inflated (e.g., $1,000/mo for 60 sq ft — fails §1.62-2(c) reasonableness)
  - Personal use of the space is documented

## Required substantiation

1. **Accountable plan written policy** (S-Corp board minutes or employment agreement establishing the plan; date must precede the first reimbursement)
2. **Square footage measurement** of home office space + photos
3. **Use log** demonstrating regular + exclusive use (calendar entries, no documented personal use of the space)
4. **Monthly reimbursement request** with substantiation (utility bills, mortgage interest, depreciation worksheet)
5. **Receipt of payment** from S-Corp to shareholder (bank transfer, check, payroll line item)
6. **S-Corp 1120-S** with reimbursement shown as Line 17 deduction
7. **Owner's Form 1040** showing the reimbursement EXCLUDED from gross income (no W-2 inclusion, no Schedule E inclusion)
8. **Pro rata calculation** of home expenses: utilities, depreciation, mortgage interest, real estate taxes, insurance — apportioned by business-use percentage per §1.280A-3

```
Part I — Information About the Position
Item 1: Form 1120-S Line 17 (Other deductions) — home office accountable
plan reimbursement
Item 2: §62(c) accountable plan reimbursement to >2% shareholder for
exclusive business use of [N] square feet of shareholder's personal
residence per §280A(c)(1)
Item 3: $[X] (annual reimbursement total)

Part II — Detailed Explanation
[S-Corp name] reimbursed its 100% (or >2%) shareholder $[X amount per month]
for the regular and exclusive use of [N] square feet of [his/her] primary
residence for business purposes pursuant to §62(c) accountable plan rules.
The reimbursement is excluded from the shareholder's wages under Treas.
Reg. §1.62-2 and the position is supported by IRS Publication 587.
Documentation supporting the accountable plan (S-Corp board minutes dated
[date] establishing the plan), square footage measurement, exclusive-use
requirement, and monthly substantiation requests are maintained by the
taxpayer. The reimbursement rate is calculated as [X% business-use
percentage] × [annual home expense pool of utilities + depreciation +
mortgage interest + insurance] per §1.280A-3 proration.
```

## Common audit-defense framing

When IRS challenges:

1. **Show the accountable plan policy** with date pre-dating first reimbursement.
2. **Show square footage measurement** + photos demonstrating exclusive use.
3. **Show no personal use** during business hours (calendar, agenda, etc.).
4. **Show pro rata calculation** of home expenses.
5. **Cite §62(c) + §1.62-2 + §280A(c)(1) + Pub 587**.
6. **Show reimbursement records** + receipt of payment.

Typical IRS questions:
- "What is the square footage of the home office?"
- "Is it used exclusively for business?"
- "When was the accountable plan policy established?"
- "How was the reimbursement amount determined?"
- "Was there any personal use of the space?"
- "Where is the substantiation pack?"

Docket pre-logs each.

## Common mis-uses / failure modes

| Mis-use | Why it fails | Penalty |
|---|---|---|
| Mixed-use space (e.g., guest room + office) | §280A(c)(1) exclusive-use test fails | §6694(a)-(b) |
| Reimbursement before accountable plan established | §1.62-2(c) requires plan in place; otherwise treated as taxable wages | §6694(a) |
| Reimbursement > pro rata home expense | §1.62-2(c) reasonableness test fails | §6694(a) |
| Form 8275 not filed | Tier 3 requires disclosure for §6662 defense | §6694(a) for the preparer |
| Failing to track substantiation per §1.62-2(d) reasonable time-period | Accountable plan converts to non-accountable; reimbursement becomes taxable | §6694(a) |
| Claiming 100% of basement when half is laundry | Substantiation fail; exclusive-use fail | §6694(a) |
| Square footage inflated | Substantiation fail | §6694(a) |
| Reimbursement to <2% shareholder | §1372 doesn't apply; different §132 fringe-benefit treatment | §6694(a) |
