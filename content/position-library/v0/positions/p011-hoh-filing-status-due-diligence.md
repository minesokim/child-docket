## Position statement

A paid preparer who claims Head of Household (HoH) filing status on a client's return MUST complete the HoH section of Form 8867 (Paid Preparer's Due Diligence Checklist) and retain documentation supporting unmarried status, qualifying-person relationship, residence, and >half-year-cost-of-home test.

## Plain-English description

HoH status is one of the most-audited filing-status determinations because the standard tax savings vs Single is significant ($21,900 standard deduction for HoH vs $14,600 single for 2024; tax bracket compression). The IRS routinely challenges HoH claims where:

- Taxpayer is recently divorced or separated
- Qualifying person is a non-traditional dependent (e.g., parent in nursing home)
- Multiple potential claimants exist (e.g., divorced parents with split custody)
- Recent address changes suggest residence-test ambiguity

**The four §2(b) HoH eligibility prongs**:

1. **Unmarried (or considered unmarried)** at end of tax year per §2(b)(2). Includes legal separation OR "considered unmarried" (spouse not in home for last 6 months + paid >half cost of home + qualifying child)
2. **Paid >half cost of keeping up home** for the tax year (rent/mortgage/utilities/property tax/insurance/food consumed in home/repairs)
3. **Qualifying person lived in home >half year** (with limited exceptions — qualifying parent doesn't need to live in home)
4. **Qualifying person is**:
   - Qualifying child per §152(c), OR
   - Qualifying relative per §152(d) (only certain relationships qualify; cousins, friends, etc. do NOT)

**Why an EA cares**: HoH is among the easiest filing-status determinations to get wrong. The §6695(g) penalty is a separate $650 from EITC/CTC/AOTC on the same return. A preparer who routinely files HoH without proper substantiation has compounding audit exposure.

## Fact pattern triggers

The Discovery agent should consider this position when:
- Form 1040 filing status = HoH (Line 1, Box 4)
- Form 8867 attached
- Recent divorce / separation indicators
- Single parent with custody claims
- Multi-generational household (parent + adult child + grandchild)
- Address change in last 12 months
- Spouse was deceased in the tax year (qualifying widow/widower may be alternative)

## The four §6695(g) due-diligence requirements applied to HoH

### Requirement 1: Knowledge / reasonable inquiry

Trigger inquiries when:
- Filing status changed from MFJ in prior year (recently divorced?)
- Address doesn't match qualifying child's residence
- Multiple claimants likely (other parent or grandparent or relative)
- Qualifying person is a parent — confirm parent passed §152(d) gross-income test ($5,050 for 2024)
- "Considered unmarried" claim — verify spouse not in home for last 6 months + qualifying child + >half cost

### Requirement 2: Computation

- Apply standard deduction ($21,900 for HoH 2024) correctly
- Apply HoH tax brackets correctly (different from Single)

### Requirement 3: Form 8867 completion

- Complete Form 8867 Part IV (HoH)
- Attach to return

### Requirement 4: Documentation retention

3 years. Items:
- Form 8867
- Records supporting cost-of-home calculation (utility bills, rent receipts, mortgage statements, property tax, insurance, repair records)
- Records supporting qualifying-person relationship
- Records supporting residency (school records, medical records for child; lease/utility for parent if applicable)
- Records of inquiries made

## Required substantiation

1. **Completed Form 8867 Part IV**
2. **Cost-of-home calculation worksheet** — itemized:
   - Rent OR mortgage interest + property tax + home insurance
   - Utilities (electric, water, gas, internet for portion used in home)
   - Repairs + maintenance
   - Food consumed in the home
   - Total annual cost; >50% paid by taxpayer required
3. **Qualifying person relationship documentation**
4. **Residency documentation** (school records, medical, lease, etc.)
5. **For "considered unmarried"**: documentation of spouse's absence for last 6 months
6. **For divorced parents**: §152(e) tiebreaker analysis if relevant
7. **For qualifying parent**: parent's gross income documentation (Social Security, pension, retirement income); confirm under §152(d)(1)(B) threshold

## Common audit-defense framing

When IRS audits HoH due-diligence:

1. **Produce Form 8867 Part IV** for each return at issue
2. **Produce cost-of-home worksheet** with supporting bills
3. **Produce relationship + residency documentation** for qualifying person
4. **Show inquiry documentation** for any return with ambiguity
5. **Show 3-year retention** intact

Typical IRS preparer-audit questions:
- "Show me Form 8867 Part IV for [client]'s [year] return."
- "Show me cost-of-home calculation."
- "Was the spouse legally separated or divorced? When?"
- "Where did the qualifying child live during the tax year? Show me the records."
- "If qualifying parent, where is the gross-income documentation?"

Docket pre-logs each.

## Common mis-uses / failure modes

| Mis-use | Why it fails | Penalty |
|---|---|---|
| Form 8867 Part IV not completed | §1.6695-2(b)(3) fails | $650/failure |
| Claiming HoH while still legally married + cohabitating | §2(c) "considered unmarried" fails | $650/failure + §6694(a) |
| Cost-of-home <50% paid by taxpayer | §2(b) prong fails | $650/failure + §6694(a) |
| Qualifying parent fails gross-income test | §152(d)(1)(B) | $650/failure + §6694(a) |
| Claiming HoH for non-qualifying-relationship person (cousin, friend, fiancé's child) | §152 relationship test fails | $650/failure + §6694(a) |
| Both divorced parents claiming HoH on same child | §152(e) tiebreaker not applied | $650/failure + §6694(a) |
| Address change suggesting residence-test ambiguity not investigated | Reasonable-inquiry requirement breached | $650/failure |
