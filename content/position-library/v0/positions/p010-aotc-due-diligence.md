## Position statement

A paid preparer who claims the American Opportunity Tax Credit (AOTC) MUST complete Form 8867 due-diligence checklist for AOTC AND retain documentation. Failure triggers a separate $650 penalty per failure.

## Plain-English description

AOTC is the most-audited college-related credit. Per IRC §25A(i), it provides up to $2,500 per eligible student (100% of first $2,000 + 25% of next $2,000 in qualified expenses) for the first 4 years of post-secondary education. 40% of the credit is refundable as Additional AOTC.

**Phaseout (2025+)**: starts at $80,000 single / $160,000 MFJ MAGI; fully phased out at $90,000 / $180,000.

**Why AOTC is high-audit-risk**:
1. **Form 1098-T mismatch**: schools report tuition payments to IRS via 1098-T; IRS cross-matches. Mismatches trigger CP2000 notices.
2. **First-4-years rule**: §25A(b)(2)(C) limits AOTC to first 4 tax years of post-secondary education. Claiming in year 5+ triggers reclassification to Lifetime Learning Credit (less valuable).
3. **Degree-seeking + half-time requirement**: §25A(b)(3) requires degree-seeking, at least half-time enrollment.
4. **Felony drug conviction bar**: §25A(b)(2)(D) — students with felony drug conviction in tax year are ineligible (preparer must inquire).
5. **Refundable portion + parent vs student claim**: who claims affects refundable amount.

**Why an EA cares**: parents with college-age dependents claim AOTC routinely. CP2000 1098-T mismatches are among the most common IRS automated correspondences. A pattern of unsupported AOTC claims triggers preparer-level §6695(g) audits.

## Fact pattern triggers

The Discovery agent should consider this position when:
- Form 1040 Line 29 (AOTC) > 0 or Form 8863 attached
- Form 8867 attached
- Dependent on return is in college (typically age 18-24 + Form 1098-T received)
- Form 1098-T from an eligible institution (verify school is on IRS list)
- Qualified expenses (tuition + required fees + required course materials per §25A(f))
- MAGI within or below phaseout range

## The four §6695(g) due-diligence requirements (Treas. Reg. §1.6695-2(b)) applied to AOTC

### Requirement 1: Knowledge / reasonable inquiry

Trigger inquiries when:
- 1098-T amount differs from what taxpayer is claiming
- Student in apparent 5th+ year of post-secondary education (graduate program? gap year extension?)
- Student attending unusual institution (non-traditional, online, foreign)
- Claimed expenses include items not qualified (e.g., room and board, transportation, optional fees, equipment)
- Felony drug conviction status not addressed
- Half-time enrollment status unclear

### Requirement 2: Computation

- Apply 100% of first $2,000 + 25% of next $2,000 = max $2,500
- Apply MAGI phaseout ($80K-$90K single / $160K-$180K MFJ)
- Apply refundable portion (40% of allowable credit)
- Coordinate with §529 distributions + scholarship adjustments (no double-counting)

### Requirement 3: Form 8867 completion

- Complete Form 8867 Part III (AOTC)
- Attach Form 8863 + Form 8867 to return

### Requirement 4: Documentation retention

3 years. Items:
- Form 8867
- Form 8863 worksheet
- Form 1098-T from each institution
- Records of qualified expenses (receipts for required books + materials)
- Evidence of degree-seeking + half-time enrollment (enrollment verification letter)
- Documentation of years already claimed AOTC (to enforce 4-year cap)

## Required substantiation (the audit-defense file)

1. **Completed Form 8867 Part III**
2. **Form 8863** with proper computation
3. **Form 1098-T** from each institution
4. **Enrollment verification** (registrar letter confirming degree-seeking + half-time)
5. **Qualified expense receipts** (tuition, required fees, required course materials)
6. **MAGI computation** if AGI near phaseout range
7. **Prior-year AOTC claim history** (to verify first-4-years rule)
8. **Felony drug conviction inquiry record** (yes/no documented)
9. **3-year retention log**

## Common audit-defense framing

When IRS audits AOTC due-diligence:

1. **Produce Form 8867 Part III** + **Form 8863** computation.
2. **Produce Form 1098-T** from the institution.
3. **Produce enrollment verification** (degree-seeking + half-time).
4. **Produce qualified-expense receipts**.
5. **Document the felony drug conviction inquiry** (yes/no, asked at the right time).
6. **Show first-4-years compliance** (prior return cross-check).

Typical IRS preparer-audit questions:
- "Show me Form 8867 Part III for [client]'s [year] return."
- "Show me 1098-T from [Institution]."
- "Show me enrollment verification."
- "Show me qualified-expense documentation."
- "How did you confirm the felony drug conviction inquiry?"
- "How did you verify this was within the first 4 years of post-secondary education?"

Docket pre-logs each.

## Common mis-uses / failure modes

| Mis-use | Why it fails | Penalty |
|---|---|---|
| Form 8867 Part III not completed | §1.6695-2(b)(3) fails | $650/failure |
| Claiming AOTC in year 5+ of post-secondary education | §25A(b)(2)(C) cap | $650/failure + §6694(a) |
| Claiming AOTC for half-time or less student | §25A(b)(3) fails | $650/failure + §6694(a) |
| Claiming AOTC + 529 distribution for same expenses | §25A(g) double-counting | $650/failure + §6694(a) |
| Including room and board as qualified expenses | §25A(f) excludes | $650/failure + §6694(a) |
| Claiming AOTC twice for same student in same year (parent + student returns) | One taxpayer per student per year | $650/failure + §6694(b) |
| Felony drug conviction not inquired | §25A(b)(2)(D) test bypassed | $650/failure |
| Claiming AOTC for foreign institution not on eligible list | §25A(f)(2) institution requirement | $650/failure + §6694(a) |
| 1098-T mismatch (preparer claims more than 1098-T amount) | CP2000 trigger; reasonable-inquiry duty | $650/failure + §6694(a) |
