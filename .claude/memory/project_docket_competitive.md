Docket's positioning vs the two big AI-native tax competitors:

**Accrual** ($75M, General Catalyst): AI that drafts returns, reads K-1s, auto-applies corrections. Targets large firms (H&R Block, Top 100). Strong on document intelligence, email ingestion, year-over-year comparison, source traceability. No client comms, no billing, no solo EA focus.

**Basis** ($100M, $1.15B valuation): AI agents for CAS/Tax/Audit. "Long horizon agents" that work autonomously. First to demo autonomous 1065 completion. Pure AI engine layer, no practice management at all.

**Docket's moat**: Neither competitor touches client communication automation, billing/payments, pipeline management, intake/organizer, or solo EA economics ($49/mo vs enterprise pricing). For Antonio, "around the return" is 70%+ of where time goes — document chasing, client comms, pipeline visibility. That's Docket's lane.

**Features to adopt from Accrual**: Dynamic binders (adaptive checklists for returning clients), document summaries with plain English, source traceability, email ingestion, YoY comparison, duplicate detection, "Ignore" option on client portal for non-applicable items.

**Why:** These are document/client intelligence features that live in Docket's practice management lane without requiring a return prep engine.

**How to apply:** When building document and intelligence features, prioritize the input layer (how data gets structured) as much as the output layer (AI insights). Insights are only as good as the data feeding them.
