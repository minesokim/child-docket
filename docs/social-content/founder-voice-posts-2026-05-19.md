```

The §183 hobby loss reclassification is the most under-talked-about
preparer risk in our segment. Here is the 9-factor test the Tax
Court actually applies.

[2/7]
1. Is the activity carried on in a businesslike manner?
   (Books and records? Separate bank account? Business license?)

2. What is the expertise of the taxpayer + advisors?

3. Time and effort expended.

[3/7]
4. Expectation of asset appreciation.

5. Success in carrying on similar activities.

6. History of income or losses.
   (3 of 5 profitable years = §183(d) safe harbor presumption.)

[4/7]
7. Amount of occasional profits earned.

8. Financial status of taxpayer.
   (Does taxpayer have W-2 income to absorb hobby losses? Red flag.)

9. Personal pleasure or recreation derived from the activity.

[5/7]
If 5+ factors weigh AGAINST profit motive, IRS likely succeeds in
reclassification.

(writing, art, photography, racing) + high W-2 income = audit magnet.

Post-TCJA (2018-2025), Schedule A miscellaneous deductions are
suspended. So hobby losses cannot even partially offset hobby income

Net effect: client's "loss" disappears entirely.

- Burrus (horse activity)

(Deduction, Perplexity for Taxes, Rally, Gelt).

[2/6]
Here is why: every "maximization" surfaces positions at the aggressive
edge of the law. Tier 3 (Reasonable Basis with 8275) and Tier 4 (MLTN).

If your AI doesn't pre-draft the 8275 disclosure for Tier 3, it just
created your §6694(a) exposure.

[3/6]
If your AI doesn't refuse below Reasonable Basis, it just surfaced
listed transactions, hobby losses, abusive Augusta rates, conservation
easement schemes.

All of these have published case law rejecting the position.

[4/6]
The §6694(b) reckless penalty is $5,000 OR 75% of fees. Triggered by
"reckless disregard."

The IRS treats "should have known" as default. AI surfacing a position
the case law has decisively rejected? That's reckless disregard.

[5/6]
Compliance-first AI flips the frame:

Default to Settled law (Tier 1).
Surface Substantial Authority (Tier 2) with cited support.
Surface Reasonable Basis (Tier 3) only with pre-drafted 8275.
Refuse below Reasonable Basis with logged reasoning.

[6/6]
The refusal log becomes your audit defense. The pre-drafted 8275
becomes your §6694 defense. The cited authority chain becomes your
§6694(b) defense.

The product is the AI that REFUSES, not the AI that maximizes.

```
The most-recognized preparer penalty in tax practice is §6695(g) —
the due-diligence penalty, $650 per failure for 2026 returns. Easy
to count, easy to audit, easy to assess.

The most consequential preparer penalty is §6694 — the unreasonable
position / understatement penalty.

§6694(a): the GREATER of $1,000 or 50% of fees.
§6694(b): the GREATER of $5,000 or 75% of fees.

A preparer charging $5,000 for a high-end return loses $2,500 per
§6694(a) finding. A pattern triggers §6694(b) at $3,750 each. Three
findings in a year = PTIN suspension proceedings.
