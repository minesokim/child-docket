## Pricing strategy for the 100-customer push

Three options. Recommend **Option C (tiered scarcity)**.

### Option A: Flat founder tier $250 for all 100

- All 100 customers at $250/mo = $25K/mo MRR = $300K ARR
- Easiest sales motion (everyone gets same offer)
- Lowest ARR ceiling

### Option B: Founder 50 + standard tier 50

- 50 × $250/mo (founder) + 30 × $1,499/mo (Small wedge) + 20 × $499/mo (Solo) = **$67K/mo MRR / $810K ARR**
- Wedge (2-10 preparer firms) sells more easily at $1,499 than solos at $250
- Different sales motions for different tiers; more complex

### Option C: Tiered scarcity (RECOMMENDED)

- First 50 firms: $250/mo (founder rate, locked for life)
- Firms 51-75: $350/mo (founder rate v2, locked for life — 30% off Solo $499)
- Firms 76-100: $400/mo (founder rate v3, locked for life — 20% off Solo $499)
- After firm 100: standard tier pricing
- **MRR at 100: ~$31K/mo / $370K ARR** (lower than B, but easier velocity than B)

**Why C wins**: scarcity tightens close velocity (every signup increases the perceived price for the next prospect); same founder-tier-feels-like-deal mechanic; ARR lands between A and B; fits the 8/1 timeline better than B (which requires switching sales motions mid-push).

**Marketing language**: "Founder tier is locked at $250/mo for the first 50 firms. [X] of 50 remaining."

**For Small-tier wedge candidates** (2-10 preparer firms with active audit exposure who can absorb $1,499): offer **both** options — $400/mo founder tier OR $1,499 Small tier. Some will pick standard tier because the unlimited active-client count + full add-on bundle is worth it for a growing firm.
