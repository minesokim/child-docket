## Q&A prep (the four objections that always come up at mid-market)

### Objection 1: "We already have TaxDome / Canopy / Karbon. Why switch?"

**Answer:**
> "You don't switch — Docket runs alongside. TaxDome / Canopy / Karbon are practice management with shallow AI bolt-ons. Docket is the AI substrate behind your existing PM tool. Specifically: our Inbox Drafter agent can write into your existing PM's email surface; our Position Framework can produce 8275 disclosures into your existing prep tool; our audit defense file replaces the workpaper layer you build manually. We're additive, not replacement. Your team keeps the PM tool they're trained on; the AI substrate runs underneath."

### Objection 2: "We need SOC 2 Type II before we can sign."

**Answer:**
> "SOC 2 Type I attestation lands Q4 2026 ($15-25K Drata engagement scoped). Type II mid-2027. Type I is the procurement-gate-clearing artifact. We can sign a contract today with a Type I delivery clause; payment milestones tied to Type I report receipt. Our 12-doc policy set is already in the codebase — your procurement team can read it today and decide if the Type I bridge is sufficient given our control posture."

### Objection 3: "What about the 50-staff firm where 30 of them are revenue-billing senior staff and only 8 are juniors? The math is different."

**Answer:**
> "The math scales with junior count, not firm size. If you have 8 juniors of whom 2-3 are doing routine work, savings is $120-180K/yr. If you have 4 juniors of whom 1-2 are doing routine work, savings is $60-100K/yr — Docket still pays for itself but at lower multiple. If you have 0 juniors doing routine work because senior staff is already doing everything, the value-prop is different — Docket frees senior staff billing time, not junior FTE cost. We'd scope that on a 30-min walkthrough."

### Objection 4: "How do we know your AI won't make a mistake that costs us a client?"

**Answer:**
> "Three layers of defense. One: every position routes through Antonio (25-year EA, 1% equity) before it ships; AI-classified positions live in a separate draft namespace that never surfaces customer-facing. Two: refusal floor below Reasonable Basis — the trust gate is structural, the AI cannot ship a position below Reasonable Basis without explicit human override. Three: every action is audit-trailed in a cryptographic chain — when the IRS asks why a position was taken, the chain answers with the IRC cite + reviewer + timestamp. We're not promising infallibility; we're promising auditable cited authority + structurally bounded scope. That's the only honest promise an AI tax tool can make to a mid-market firm."
