## Slide 3 — What §6695(g) really costs you (~5 min)

**Visual**: Penalty math table

```
THE NUMBERS YOU PROBABLY HAVE NOT INTERNALIZED

§6695(g) due-diligence penalty:        $650 per failure (2026)

The full preparer-penalty stack:

§6694(b) willful/reckless           $5,000 or 75% of fees
§6695(a)-(e) procedural             $60-$650 each
§6701 aiding understatement         $1,000 / $10,000 corp
Audit defense engagement (real)     40-100 hrs @ your rate
PTIN suspension                     career-ending
```

**Antonio's speaker notes (3-4 min)**:
> "Let me walk through what these actually cost.
>
> The §6695(g) — you all know that one. $650 per failure, $2,600 max per return. Per Rev. Proc. 2025-32 for 2026 returns. Up from $635 last year.
>
> The §6694(a) — this is the one most preparers don't think enough about. It hits when you take a position without Substantial Authority and you don't disclose it via Form 8275. The penalty is the GREATER of $1,000 or 50% of your fees for that return. Think about that — if you charged $500 for a return and the position fails, you pay $1,000. If you charged $2,000, you pay $1,000. But if you charged $5,000, you pay $2,500. The greater-of-$1,000-or-50% structure is what makes this scary at the high-fee end.
>
> The §6694(b) — same structure but $5,000 or 75% of fees, for reckless or willful disregard. This is the one that gets your PTIN suspended in patterns.
>
> The §6701 — aiding penalty. $1,000 per return if you 'knew or should have known' a position was wrong. The IRS uses 'should have known' as the default — meaning if you've been doing this 10 years, you should know.
>
> Audit defense engagements — 40 to 100 hours at your hourly realization rate. At $250/hour, that's $10K to $25K of YOUR time per audit. Per audit.
>
> PTIN suspension — pattern of §6695(g) findings can trigger preparer-suspension proceedings. I've watched colleagues lose their PTIN over patterns of carelessness, not malice.
>
> So the question I'm asking is: what would you pay annually to systematically prevent all of these? Hold that question."
