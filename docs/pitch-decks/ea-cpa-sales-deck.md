## Q&A prep (the four objections that always come up)

### Objection 1: "How is this different from what TaxDome/Canopy/Karbon already does?"

**Answer:**
> "TaxDome and Canopy are practice management — they organize the workflow. They've shipped some AI features but their substrate is aging and the AI sits on top, not in the substrate. Docket is AI-native from the foundation up. The position framework, the audit chain, the trust gate, the cited authority — that's not a feature on top of a CRM, that's the architecture. Different category."

### Objection 2: "What if your AI is wrong on a position?"

**Answer:**
> "Two layers of defense. One: every position routes through Antonio — my on-platform tax advisor with 25 years EA practice and 1% equity in Docket. AI doesn't ship a position to your return; Antonio's reviewed and signed off on it. Two: contracted backup advisors from AICPA + NAEA networks provide scale-validation when Antonio's bandwidth is constrained. Every Position Library entry has a `reviewedBy` field with the EA or CPA's name + license number + sign-off timestamp. AI-classified positions live in a separate draft namespace that never surfaces to a customer-facing surface. We don't promise infallibility — we promise human-reviewed cited authority on every position."

### Objection 3: "What about data security? My clients' SSNs are in there."

**Answer:**
> "Per-tenant data encryption keys, AES-256-GCM with AAD binding so even if our database leaked, the data is unreadable without the per-tenant key. Cryptographic audit chain so every action is tamper-evident. RLS enforcement at the database level so tenant data can't cross. MFA required. 12-doc SOC 2 Type II policy set already documented. SOC 2 Type I attestation in flight for Q4 2026 (the bridge to Type II by mid-2027). We have a published Coverage Map and a published security posture. None of which I expect you to read tonight — but it's all there if you want to."

### Objection 4: "Why should I trust an AI tool with my PTIN?"

**Answer:**
> "You shouldn't — and that's the framing. Docket is not a replacement for your professional judgment. It's a defense layer behind your judgment. You decide every position. Docket cites primary authority, classifies confidence tier, drafts 8275 disclosures, and refuses anything below Reasonable Basis. The AI never speaks to your client. The AI never auto-files a return. You're at the edge approving outcomes. That's the L9 design lock. Your judgment is the moat against TurboTax commoditization, and Docket arms it instead of replacing it."
