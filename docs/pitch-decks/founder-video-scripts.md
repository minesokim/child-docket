# Founder Video Scripts — Vory-style 9-beat structure adapted

> *Two cuts: 60-sec YC application + 2-min general pitch. Same 9-beat structure.*
> *Style anatomy borrowed from Vory's pitch video (rhetorical "why X" cold open → macro reframe via miniaturization → one-sentence TAM → day-in-the-life pain walk → shock stat punchline → name + vision tagline → 3 integrated systems with parallel naming → zoom out to ecosystem stakes → founder origin + traction + vision close).*
> *Locked 2026-05-11 wedge framing (2-10 preparer firms; Antonio = founding firm; solo as expansion ladder, not ICP).*

---

## Why the 9-beat structure works

It's nine moves in 2 minutes (or six moves in 60 seconds). The order matters. Each beat earns the right to the next:

1. **Rhetorical "why X" cold open** — a question the listener was already half-asking
2. **Macro reframe via miniaturization** — "Opportunity is huge, but the way to see it is to get small." Earns the right to be specific.
3. **One-sentence TAM claim** that immediately recontextualizes the category. No deck, no chart.
4. **Day-in-the-life pain walk** — narrative, not bullets. The listener watches the system fail in real time.
5. **One shock stat as punchline** + back-room snapshot + margin/penalty kill
6. **Name + vision tagline in one breath** — the borrowed metaphor that does the work of a paragraph
7. **Three integrated systems with parallel naming** — same prefix three times is the whole reason it lands
8. **Zoom out to ecosystem stakes** — the product saves more than the customer
9. **Founder origin + traction + vision close** — personal heritage earns the mission. Numbers prove it's real. Final line restates the tagline.

## 60-Second YC Application Video Script

> *Target: 60 seconds. Practice 30 times. Hit 58-62s consistently before record.*
> *David on camera the entire time. Plain background. No music. No b-roll over the speech — visual is your face + the words. Optional final 3 seconds: cut to Haokun + David on a couch with a laptop for the "we built it" beat.*

---

**(0:00) Beat 1+2 — Why tax + macro reframe:**

> A lot of people ask why tax. The opportunity is enormous, but the way to see it is to sit next to one preparer for one week.

**(0:08) Beat 3+4 — TAM claim + day-in-the-life pain walk (compressed):**

> Tax prep is a $14 billion service industry that runs on inboxes and spreadsheets. The average enrolled agent does 300 returns a season on a Jenga tower of Drake, OLT, email, SMS, and a portal nobody logs into. A W-2 arrives by text on Tuesday. A 1099 shows up in a voicemail on Friday. Nothing connects. They retype it all into software built in 1996.

**(0:30) Beat 5 — Shock stat + margin kill:**

> They miss one due-diligence question on a child tax credit, and the IRS assesses $650 per failure, up to $2,600 from a single return. The median solo preparer nets less than $80K a year.

**(0:42) Beat 6+7 — Name + vision tagline + three systems (compressed to one line):**

> Docket is the AI defense layer that runs the tax practice. Three integrated systems: a System of Record that consolidates every document, message, and transcript into one client memory; a System of Defense that cites primary authority on every position and generates the audit defense file as a side effect; a System of Action that runs the triage queue, the prep workflow, and the review pipeline.

**(0:54) Beat 9 — Traction + vision close (Beat 8 cut for time):**

> My founding firm — Antonio Vazquez, EA in California — is defending two active IRS audits using Docket right now. Founder tier is locked at $250 a month for the first 50 firms, less than half of one preparer penalty. We're building the defense layer behind every tax professional in America.

**(1:00) End.**

---

### Critical discipline notes for the 60-sec take

- **Pace**: ~160 words/min. The script above is ~155 words. Tight but achievable.
- **Cuts you cannot make**: $14B / $650 / 300 returns / $250/mo / "two active IRS audits" / "AI defense layer" / "founder tier". Every number does work.
- **Cuts you can make if running long**: "The average enrolled agent does 300 returns a season" (lose this if you have to). Don't cut the pain walk; cut the throat-clearing.
- **The final 5 seconds**: "the defense layer behind every tax professional in America" is the tagline restatement. Practice landing it. Eye contact with camera through the period.
- **The first 8 seconds**: "A lot of people ask why tax" — pause for 1 second after this line. Forces the listener to lean in. The Vory open does this and it works.
- **Haokun's slot**: optional 3-second cut at the very end ("Built by David and Haokun, partners for five years, shipping from Riverside"). Decide based on whether you have time. If under 58s, add it. If at 62s+, cut.
- **Do not say**: "AI-powered" / "revolutionary" / "next-generation" / "save you time." All dead language to a YC reviewer.
- **Do say**: "$650 per failure" verbatim. "Two active IRS audits" verbatim. "Founder tier locked" verbatim. The verbatim numbers are the trust signal.

## 2-Minute General Pitch Video Script

> *For Anthropic Startup Program / Forum / Mucker / Pear / Neo / press / conference. The longer form lets you breathe through Beat 8 (ecosystem stakes) which the 60-sec version cuts.*
> *Target: 2:00-2:15. Practice 20 times. Hit 2:05-2:10 consistently before record.*

---

**(0:00) Beat 1+2 — Why tax + macro reframe:**

> A lot of people ask me, why tax. It's a fair question. The opportunity is enormous, but the way to see it is to sit next to one preparer for one week.

**(0:10) Beat 3+4 — TAM claim + day-in-the-life pain walk:**

> Tax prep is a $14 billion service industry that runs on inboxes and spreadsheets. The average enrolled agent or small-firm CPA does 300 returns a season on a Jenga tower of Drake, OLT, email, SMS, a client portal nobody logs into, and a notepad on the desk.
>
> A W-2 arrives by text message on Tuesday. A 1098 shows up in a voicemail on Wednesday. The spouse emails a 1099 on Friday. Nothing connects. The preparer retypes it all into software built in 1996.

**(0:45) Beat 5 — Shock stat + back-room snapshot + margin kill:**

> They miss one due-diligence question on a child tax credit, and the IRS assesses a §6695(g) penalty — six hundred and fifty dollars per failure, up to twenty-six hundred dollars from a single return. The median solo preparer nets less than eighty thousand dollars a year.
>
> And meanwhile TurboTax spent ninety million dollars last year telling their clients they don't need a human at all.

**(1:05) Beat 6 — Name + vision tagline:**

> Docket is the AI defense layer that runs the practice and arms the preparer. Built specifically for the side of the desk where the personal PTIN risk lives — the 2-to-10-preparer firms the funded AI tax companies can't economically serve.

**(1:20) Beat 7 — Three integrated systems with parallel naming:**

> Three integrated systems.
>
> One: Docket System of Record — consolidates every document, transcript, call, text, and portal message into one Universal Client Memory.
>
> Two: Docket System of Defense — generates the position framework, the 8275 disclosure, and the due-diligence evidence chain that survives an IRS audit.
>
> Three: Docket System of Action — runs the triage queue, the prep workflow, the review pipeline, and the client portal that actually gets used.

**(1:45) Beat 8 — Zoom out to ecosystem stakes:**

> Docket is the operating system that runs the tax practice and arms the people running it. When one small tax practice stays in business, the small business across the street keeps its bookkeeper. The family down the block keeps their refund. The local economy keeps the only financial advisor most of them will ever have.

**(2:00) Beat 9 — Founder origin + traction + vision close:**

> My co-founder Haokun and I have been working together for five years. Our founding firm is Antonio Vazquez, an enrolled agent in California — he's defending two active IRS audits using Docket right now. Founder tier locked at two hundred fifty dollars a month for the first fifty firms. Our vision is to put a defense layer behind every tax professional in America, so the people who actually know their clients win the next decade. Not the platforms trying to replace them.

**(2:15) End.**

---

### Critical discipline notes for the 2-min take

- **Pace**: ~155 words/min. Script is ~340 words for ~2:10 spoken at deliberate pace.
- **The pause after the cold open**: "A lot of people ask me, why tax." [PAUSE 1.5 SEC] "It's a fair question." The pause is the hook. Practice it.
- **Beat 5 timing**: the §6695(g) penalty number lands at 0:55-1:00. That's the slap. Don't rush it. Say "six hundred and fifty dollars per failure" with the numbers spelled out — written-out numbers slow the listener and let the figure land. Same trick Vory uses with "$82 billion."
- **Beat 7 — the parallel construction**: "System of Record" / "System of Defense" / "System of Action." Same prefix three times. The structure carries the listener through what would otherwise be 90 seconds of feature explanation in 30. Do not break the parallelism by adding qualifiers ("System of Record, which consolidates..."). Land each name first, *then* the function.
- **Beat 8 — the ecosystem zoom**: this is where Docket gets emotional. The Vory equivalent is "when one [grocery store] opens, a restaurant opens next door." For Docket: small tax practice → bookkeeper next door → family's refund → local financial advisor. The listener feels the local economy stakes, not just the customer.
- **The personal beat**: you have a UCR-CS-shipping-from-Riverside story. Don't apologize for it. The Vory founder had a "grandparents in grocery" line; you have "co-founder Haokun and I have been working together for five years" — same purpose, different content.
- **The close**: "Not the platforms trying to replace them." This is your fight line. Land it with the camera. Last 5 seconds.

## What's different from Vory's script + why

| Beat | Vory's version | Docket's version | Why different |
|---|---|---|---|
| 3 (TAM) | "Grocery: bigger than restaurants, bigger than hotels" | "$14 billion service industry that runs on inboxes and spreadsheets" | Tax-prep TAM is more specific than grocery's; the surface-area framing ("inboxes + spreadsheets") does the same recontextualization work without needing the larger comparison |
| 5 (shock stat) | "$82B in out-of-stocks" | "$650 per failure × up to 4 = $2,600 from one return" | Vory's stat is industry-level pain; Docket's is preparer-level pain. The preparer hears their own bank account in the number, not the industry's. |
| 6 (tagline) | "Self-driving supermarket" | "AI defense layer that runs the practice and arms the preparer" | Self-driving is the borrowed metaphor for the autonomy-of-store; defense layer is the borrowed metaphor for the protect-the-PTIN value. Different physics. |
| 7 (three systems) | Record / Action / Transaction | Record / Defense / Action | We don't have a "Transaction" pillar at Docket's scale yet (Path 2 will eventually but the System of Transaction framing matches Vory's payments revenue 60% of their model). Our Defense pillar carries the IP that Vory's Action carries. |
| 8 (ecosystem) | "When one grocery opens, restaurant opens, bank opens, coffee shop opens" | "When one small tax practice stays in business, bookkeeper survives, family keeps refund, local financial advisor stays" | Same emotional arc; different industry stakes |
| 9 (origin) | "My grandparents were in grocery. My parents met in a supermarket." | "Haokun and I have been working together for five years. Founding firm Antonio defending two IRS audits." | We don't have multi-generational heritage; we have a real co-founder + real customer in real audit defense. That's the substitute. |

## What to lock before filming

1. **Pricing math citation**: §6695(g) is $650 per failure for 2026 returns. If filming after IRS Rev. Proc. 2026-XX publishes 2027 inflation adjustments, update to the new figure (~$670 estimate).
2. **Antonio's audit count**: "two active IRS audits" — verify still active at film time. If resolved, update to "Antonio defended two IRS audits using Docket; both resolved without preparer penalty assessment" (stronger if the audits closed favorably).
3. **Founder-50 slot count**: "first fifty firms" stays; "founder tier locked at $250" stays. The slot count "X of 50 remaining" goes on the deck slide, not the video.
4. **Co-founder visible at minute 2:00**: Haokun on camera for the personal beat. Decide if both founders on camera throughout or only the close.
5. **Background**: outdoor or in-office, plain. NOT a Zoom tile in a grid. NOT a stock-photo office. Real space.
6. **No music in the final cut.** Vory's video has none. The pacing is the rhythm.
7. **Film 6 weeks before YC deadline.** Allow time to re-shoot if any beat doesn't land. Mid-June latest.

## Filming checklist (the day-of discipline)

- [ ] Outline taped to camera's side (out of frame). Don't memorize-and-pray. Reading from a beat-list with eye-contact-up keeps the pacing right.
- [ ] Pre-roll for 3 seconds before saying anything ("rolling, rolling..." energy)
- [ ] One full take, then ten more for safety. The keeper is usually take 4-7.
- [ ] Watch back take 5. Time it. If it's under 58s or over 62s on the 60-sec version, the script needs trimming or padding before take 6.
- [ ] Sound check before any take — phone or mic. Audio quality matters more than 4K video.
- [ ] Antonio quote / testimonial line possible if you want a 5-sec interruption ("My founding firm — Antonio — said in a call last week: 'this is the first AI tool I'd let near my PTIN.'") Don't add unless you have permission + a real Antonio quote.

## What NOT to put in the founder video

Per CLAUDE.md voice rules + the YC reviewer reality:

- ❌ "AI-powered" / "AI-native" / "AI-first" — dead language for YC at this point
- ❌ "Revolutionary" / "game-changing" / "next-generation" — AI vocabulary that scrubs on sight
- ❌ "We're disrupting [X]" — disrupt is dead language
- ❌ Stock photos / decorative gradients / neural-network imagery in any b-roll
- ❌ Background music
- ❌ Multiple-take cuts that hide where you re-recorded — visible editing is the bug
- ❌ Mention of "solo EAs" as the ICP — wedge is 2-10 preparer firms per the 2026-05-11 lock
- ❌ Apology for being early-stage / two-founder / Riverside-based — those are advantages, not gaps to disclaim
- ❌ Detailed product feature list — three systems is the limit. Adding a fourth dilutes the parallel construction.
